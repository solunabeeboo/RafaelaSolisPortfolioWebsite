About Me

Game Developer & Designer

Hi! I'm Rafaela Solis, a passionate game developer dedicated to creating engaging, immersive, and emotional player-focused gaming experiences. My journey in game development started as a tiny baby. My father started a company during the tech boom, and performed IT services for the city of Austin, Texas. He always had every kind of computer, and quite often we would sit down together in front of a 4 inch thick laptop to play obscure cobbled together kids games that my tiny brain could understand. I've been hooked ever since.

I major in Game Design and Game Programming at Champlain College (2028). I have specific focus in gameplay programming, implementation, system design, level design, technical design, narrative design, rapid concepting, rapid prototyping, etc. I have experience working with Unity, Unreal Engine, C#, C++, Python, Java, and Blueprints. I don't just love the games, I love the puzzle of making people happy, so I've worked on a plethora of genres: Maze-Puzzle, Action-Adventure, Educational, Drawing-Combat, Multiplayer-Coop/Competitive, and more! I'm driven by the beautiful things we as people are able to create, and the process it takes to get there, the emergent or focused narratives, the experiences, the player flow.

Throughout my career, I've worked on two non-academic large-team projects: Mega Market Mayhem and Scriptorium. Both are in their demo stages, with Scriptorium in active development. I've done dozens of indie / solo games for myself and jams, and a plethora of prototypes for the academic setting. Each project has taught me valuable lessons about teamwork, project management, game design, scope, limitations, and the importance of the player.
